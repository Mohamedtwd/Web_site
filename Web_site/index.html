The blue city

Have you enjoyed your holiday

You will be amazed if you participate in a tour of the city of Chefchaouen, which is called the Blue City. The city of Chefchaouen was called the “Blue City” because of its ancient tradition of using the color blue to color the buildings and houses in the old city. This tradition is believed to go back to Spanish times when the city was under Spanish control in the 15th and 16th centuries. It is believed that the color blue was used to repel mosquitoes or to cool homes during the hot summer. Over time, the color blue became part of the city's architectural and cultural identity, thus earning it the nickname "Blue City".

The Beautiful city

The capital of Souss

Agadir, a coastal city located in southern Morocco, is famous for its beautiful sandy beaches and ideal location next to the High Atlas Mountains. It is considered a great tourist destination where visitors can enjoy the sun, sea and a variety of water activities. The city also has lively markets and restaurants serving traditional Moroccan cuisine, making it an ideal destination to relax and discover the local culture.

MOROCCAN CULTURE

Our culture, Our pride

Moroccan culture is distinguished by its unique diversity, which combines Arab, Amazigh, African and Islamic elements. Moroccan culture reflects a rich history of cultural exchange and multiple influences, evident in works of art, architecture, music, literature and cuisine. Social solidarity and hospitality are an essential part of Moroccan culture, contributing to the formation of the social and cooperative spirit of society.

AMAZIGH CULTURE

Our culture, Our pride

Berber culture is considered one of the oldest in all of North and North Africa, and includes a rich heritage of feuds and arts. One of the main elements of this culture is the Berber language, which is branched into many dialects. Traditional arts such as weaving, sculpture and music are mainly Berber, and reflect a rich heritage of intricate designs and patterns. In addition, the Berbers chose for their unique social and religious traditions, which include celebrations of their seasons and holidays, which were said about them and their societal values.
